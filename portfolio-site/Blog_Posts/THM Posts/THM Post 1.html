This post offers a general walkthrough of the TryHackMe web application. We'll explore the dashboard and highlight key sections that support learning, tracking progress, and launching labs. If you're here for hands-on content, feel free to jump ahead to the learning paths. But if you want a quick tour of the platform's core functions—read on.

Field NotesTryHackMe

../Landing Page: Part 1

The landing page is where you will be brought on login. It contains a large amount of information and is broken up between the nav bar and the card body. I will start with the Nav Bar and work my way down.

  • Dashboard - This is the landing page where you will be taken each time you log in.
  • Learn - This houses this web apps learning applications.
  • Practice - Where you will find hands on training environments like the SOC Sumulator.
  • Compete - Here you will find your standing in the weekly leagues, leaderboard and King of the Hill.
  • Search - To search the web apps for a particular word or phrase.
  • Notification - Here you will receive notifications like league standings, Badges and Certificate awards.
  • Streak Tracker - This tracks your active days on THM.
  • Profile Image - This is a link to your settings, profile and more.

Below the Nav is the body of the web page. Here you will find individual cards for each section of this page. I will be going from left to right and then top to bottom.

  • Banner Card - This card in my case provides a link to active challenges to help ingrain your learning. I am not sure if this is the same banner card that is shown for everyone.
  • A Greeting Message  - This is tied to your actual name that you entered in sign up and is found in your profile and not the username you entered. I have blurred mine out for privacy.
  • Discord Server CTA - A simple link that will bring you to their discord server.
  • Workspaces - I have not tried this functionality yet. Seems like a co-op style room where you can work with multiple people on a single room. Likely for use in training with your team.
  • My Learning - Allowing you to view the status of the current room you are enrolled in, recent rooms you have been enrolled in and any saved rooms you may have.
  • Table Top Exercises - I have not tried this functionality yet either. Seems to be a simulated red team exercise to test your teams OODA Loop. 
  • League Card - This shows your current league standings. 

../Landing_Page:Part_2

This is the bottom half of the card body. 

  • New Rooms - This shows rooms that have been recently created. Likely very useful in later stages when you have completed the learning paths and cleared most of the rooms that interest you...or most of the rooms in total.
  • Questions - Shows how many questions you have answered this week. I am not sure if this aggregates all questions between learning paths and rooms/challenges or if this is specific to learning paths only.
  • Stats - This shows your current status in total.
  • Skill Matrix - This aggregates your learning, scores it and gives you a title. It even provides a matrix to show where your strengths are leaning most. This seems to be based on the learning rooms you have completed and the content therein. Not necessarily on demonstrated strengths. Unless it also aggregates room/challenge completion status. If they are including the challenge/room completion status, I would be interested to see how they weight the data.

../Learn:PArt_1

Roadmap is the landing tab of the Learn page. This is where you will be introduced to the more structured learning paths available with THM. Here you have a single entry point that then branches into three separate paths.   
    
Intro: Computer Science Basics – This provides a very remedial understanding of everything from Operating Systems, to Network Topology and beyond. If you do not know about the different types of common network topologies, how packets are created and managed or how the OSI model works. I highly recommend you start here.
        
Cyber Security 101 – Here we dive into a more in-depth version of the previous module. This covers not only more in-depth networking and OS concepts but also; different types of command line, Defensive and Offensive tooling and much more.

  • Computer Science Basics (Intro) - This provides a very remedial understanding of everything from Operating Systems to Network Topology and beyond. if you do not know about the different types of common network topologies, how packets are created and managed or how the OSI model works. I highly recommend you start here.
  • Cyber Security 101 - Here we dive deep into a more in-depth version of the previous module. This covers not only more in-depth networking and OS concepts but also; different types of commend line, defensive and offensive tools and much more.

../Paths

Paths Provides access to each room found in the previously discussed Roadmap. This is a less structured option and allows you to search for specific content you want to learn. You can search these rooms based on keywords, difficulty rating and whether you have not started them, started them or completed them.

../Modules

Modules allows you to filter down your learning based on your specific tasks. Tasks covered include; Beginner to Advanced Splunk, Pentesting Skills, Injection Attacks, Incident Response and more.

../Walkthroughs

Walkthroughs are detailed user guides to almost every cyber security tool or subject you can think of. It is searchable and filterable to an almost insane degree.

../Networks

Networks I am not super familiar with as I have not used it yet. However, it appears to be a curated list of challenge rooms that utilize vulnerable networks.

../PRactice

Provides a drop down menu that allows you to access pages specific to;
        
Challenges
SOC Simulator
Threat Hunting Simulator
Get Certified

I have not personally used any of these pages yet, but they seem fairly self explanatory.

../Compete

Provides a menu that allows you to access the following pages

  • Weekly Leagues - A competitive ranking for weekly activity.
  • Leaderboard - A competitive ranking that can be filtered by all time, regular and by geographical location.
  • King of the Hill - A competitive ranking for king of the hill style games.

../Summary:

This is a well laid out and user friendly web application. It provides quick access to the most sought after information. The breakdown of learning from structured paths to a more - à la carte style searchable index is fantastic. The variety of challenge rooms and other resources to allow you to
test and iterate on your learning is amazing. I look forward to spending many more hours here.

As someone transitioning from risk management into cyber security, I find platforms like THM invaluable—not just for the content, but for the way they simulate real-world environments. This walkthrough is part of my broader documentation strategy: to reflect not just what I learn, but how I learn.
